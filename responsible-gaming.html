4. Recognizing Problem Gambling

Gambling should be a form of entertainment, not a way to make money. If you are concerned about your gambling habits or those of a loved one, consider these questions:

  • Do you spend more money or time on gambling than you intended?
  • Do you chase losses, trying to win back money you’ve lost?
  • Does gambling interfere with your work, studies, or personal life?
  • Have you tried to stop gambling but been unable to?
  • Do you feel restless or irritable when you try to cut down or stop gambling?
  • Are you borrowing money or selling possessions to gamble?
  • Are you hiding your gambling from family and friends?
  • Do you gamble to escape problems or feelings of anxiety/depression?

If you answered yes to several of these questions, you might be experiencing a gambling problem.
